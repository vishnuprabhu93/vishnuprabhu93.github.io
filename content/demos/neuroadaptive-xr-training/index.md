---
title: "Neuroadaptive Extended Reality for Clinical Training"
date: 2026-07-09
---

Clinical training in extended reality becomes far more powerful when the scenario can sense how
the trainee is coping and respond to it. Instead of a fixed script that plays out identically
regardless of who is in the headset, a *neuroadaptive* scenario continuously infers cognitive load
and stress from physiological signals — heart-rate variability, electrodermal activity, and
pupillometry — and adjusts what the trainee encounters next.

The demo below makes that closed loop visible. A simulated clinical scene runs against a scrolling
readout of synthetic biosensor traces. A transparent, inspectable rule combines those signals into
a single load index and sorts it into a regulation band. When the loop is adaptive, the scenario
responds: easing difficulty and offering scaffolding when the trainee is overloaded, and raising
the challenge when they are calmly in control. Toggle adaptation off to watch the same run play out
as a fixed script, inject a complication to perturb the system, and switch between
emergency-medicine, pediatric-readiness, and surgical-education domains.
